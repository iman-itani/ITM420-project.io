Use-Case Name:
Add service

Description:
The process of adding a new service involves the administrator filling out a form with the service's details, submitting it, verifying the information, creating a new service record, and notifying relevant parties within the medical center.
 
Precondition:
Must have the necessary permissions and access to the system's management interface to add a new service
Trigger:
 Identification of a need or opportunity for a new service within the system.
This trigger can come from various sources, such as patient requests, market research, or internal business strategies.
Typical Course Of Events:
Actor
System response

 
 Step 1: the administrator accesses the system's management interface and navigates to the section for adding new services.

Step 3: The administrator fills out the form with the necessary information and submits it.
Step 6: check the new service in the system's service catalog and make any necessary adjustments to its settings.
 
 Step 2: The interface presents a form for entering the details of the new service, including its name, description, pricing, and any other relevant information.
Step 4: The system processes the request and verifies that all required fields have been completed correctly.
Step 5: Once the request is successfully submitted and validated, the system creates a new service record and adds it to the list of medical services available.
Step 7: The cashier is notified with the changes.
Alternate Courses:
 Alternate to step 4:  The data entered by the administrator is invalid.
    • The system displays an error message indicating that the data entered is invalid.
    • The administrator re-enters the data.
    • The use-case continues from step 3.
Conclusion:
Upon successful completion of the use-case, a new service type is added to the system, which can be used by the cashier while recording the patient's transactions.
Postcondition:
 The new service type is stored in the system and can be used for future transactions.
The new service is properly promoted and marketed to patients.
Business Rules:
  • The system must comply with data protection laws and regulations.
  • The system must have a mechanism to authenticate the user before adding a new service type.
  • Only authorized personnel should be able to add a new service type.
Implementation Constraints and Specifications:
The system must have a backup and recovery mechanism in case of data loss or system failure.
Assumptions:
 The details entered by the administrator are accurate and complete.
The system is capable of storing and processing large amounts of data.
