Use-Case Name:
Issue payroll for contractual employees
 Use Case Type
Business Requirements þ
Use-Case ID:
Priority:
Source:
Interview 2
Primary System Actor:
Accountant
Primary Business Actor:
Other Participating Actors:
Other Interested Stakeholders:
Contractual employees
Description:
The use-case describes the process of calculating and issuing payroll for the contractual employees on the 20th of each month. This process involves gathering data related to the contractual employees, including the number of hours worked, hourly rate, and other compensation details.
Precondition:
The contractual employees must have completed their work hours for the month, and their timesheets must be available in the system.
Trigger:
The 20th of each month.
Typical Course of Events:
System response

 
 Step 1: The system retrieves the contractual employees' data, including hours worked, hourly rate, and other compensation details using extract data use case.
Step 2: The system calculates the contractual employees' salaries based on the data retrieved.
Alternate Courses:
Alt step 1: If there is a discrepancy in the contractual employees' timesheets, the accountant notifies the human resources department to resolve the issue.
Conclusion:
The payroll for the contractual employees is issued accurately and on time.
Postcondition:
The contractual employees receive their salaries and pay slips.
Business Rules:
 The contractual employees' timesheets must be approved by their respective managers before being processed for payroll.
The contractual employees' hourly rates and compensation details must be up to date in the system.
Implementation Constraints and Specifications:
 The system must integrate with the time tracking software to gather the contractual employees' timesheet data.
The system must comply with the company's payroll policies and regulations.
Assumptions:
 The contractual employees' timesheets are accurate and approved.
The payroll administrator has access to the system and all the necessary data to issue the payroll.
