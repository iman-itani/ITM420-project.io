Use-Case Name:
Modify Record
Use Case Type
Business Requirements þ
Use-Case ID:
Priority:
Source:
Interview 2
Primary System Actor:
Cashier
Primary Business Actor:
Other Participating Actors:
 Database (external server)
Administrator (External receiver)
Other Interested Stakeholders:
Description:
Modify previous records in the system related to the patient's information, physician's name, type of service received, payments made by patients, and donations from donors.
Precondition:
The patient should have an existent record in the database system.
Trigger:
A change was made to the transaction, for example the patients’ contribution changed due to increase in the donor’s contribution.
Typical Course Of Events:
Actor
System response

 
 Step 1: The cashier logs in to the system using valid login credentials.
Step 2: The cashier selects the option to modify a record.
Step 4:  The cashier enters the search criteria such as the patient's name or patient ID to retrieve the transaction details of the patient using Extract data use case
Step 7: The cashier makes the necessary modifications to the record and saves it.
Step 8: The cashier states the reason that triggered the modification.
 Step 3:  The system prompts the cashier or administrator to search for the record they want to modify.
Step 5:  The system displays the record that matches the search criteria.
Step 6: The system allows the cashier or administrator to modify the relevant fields such as patient information, physician's name, type of service received, payments made by patients, and donations from donors.
Step 9: The system validates the modifications made and displays a confirmation message indicating that the record has been successfully modified.
Step 10: The system reports the change that took place to the administrator.
Alternate Courses:
  • Alternate to step 5:  The record cannot be found.
    • The system displays an error message indicating that the record cannot be found.
    • The use-case continues from step 4.
  • Alternate to step 9: The data entered by the cashier or administrator is invalid.
    • The system displays an error message indicating that the data entered is invalid.
    • The use-case continues from step 7
Conclusion:
Upon successful completion of the use-case, the selected record is modified in the system, which includes changes to patient information, physician's name, type of service received, payments made by patients, and donations from donors
Postcondition:
The modified record is stored in the system and can be accessed by authorized personnel for future reference.
Business Rules:
  • Cashier needs to report the reason that triggered the modification in the case of modified transactions.
  • Only authorized personnel should be able to modify records.
    •  
    Implementation Constraints and Specifications:
    The system must have a backup and recovery mechanism in case of data loss or system failure.
    Assumptions:
    The modifications made by the cashier or administrator are accurate and complete.
