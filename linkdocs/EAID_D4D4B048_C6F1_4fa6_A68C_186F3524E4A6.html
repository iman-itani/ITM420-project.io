Description:
This use case involves generating invoices for buyers who purchase items from the medical center's stock. The invoice includes details such as the item name, quantity, unit price, total price, and the buyer's information.
Precondition:
The medical center has items available in the stock to sell.
Trigger:
The accountant receives a request from the administration to generate invoice.
Typical Course of Events:
Actor
System response

 
 Step 1: The accountant logs onto the system to generate the invoice.
Step 3: The accountant enters details such as invoice number, invoice issue date, receiver name (business contact information in case the receiver is a business B2B), terms of payment, description of good(s), quantity, item(s) code, item(s) name, and signature.
Step 5: The accountant sends/prints out the invoice to the receiver to sign it.
 Step 2: The system displays the invoice template that needs to be filled.
Step 4: The system verifies the validity of the entered details and issues the invoice.
Step 6: The system saves a copy of the invoice (to be used in future reports).
Alternate Courses:
Alternate step 6: If the buyer disputes the details on the invoice, the accountant investigates the issue and resolves it with the buyer.
Conclusion:
The invoice is issued to the buyer and the transaction is recorded in the system.
Postcondition:
The stock quantity of the item is updated, the buyer has received the item, and the transaction is recorded in the system.
Business Rules:
Only authorized personnel can issue invoices and record transactions.
Implementation Constraints and Specifications:
 The system must have a feature to generate invoices.
The system must have a feature to record transactions.
The invoices must be sent to the buyer in a timely manner.
Assumptions:
The buyer will pay the total amount on the invoice.
