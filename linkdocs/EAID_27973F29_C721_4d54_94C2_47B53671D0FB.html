Use-Case Name:
Enter Data
Use Case Type

Use-Case ID:
Priority:
Source:
Interview 1
Primary System Actor:
Cashier
Primary Business Actor:
Description:
Enter information related to the physician's name, type of service received, payments made by patients and donations from donors
Precondition:
The cashier must have a valid login credential to access the system and must be authorized to enter record data.
Trigger:

The patient wants to benefit from a medical service at IMC.
Typical Course of Events:
Actor
System response

 
 Step 1: The cashier logs in to the system using valid login credentials.
Step 2: The cashier selects the option to enter a new record.
Step 5: The cashier enters the physician's name, type of service received, payments made by patients, and donations from donors into the system.
Step 8: The cashier confirms the accuracy of the record.
Step 3: the cashier enters the patient ID to retrieve the other patient information using Extract data use case
Step 4: The system prompts the cashier to enter the physician's name, type of service received, payments made by patients, and donations from donors
Step 6: The system validates the data entered by the cashier and saves it in the database.
Step 7: The system displays a confirmation message indicating that the record has been successfully saved.
 
Alternate Courses:
 Alternate to step 5: if the data entered by the cashier is invalid,
Step 1: The system displays an error message indicating that the data entered is invalid.
The use-case continues from step 4
Conclusion:
Upon successful completion of the use-case, a new record is added to the system, which includes the physician's name, type of service received, payments made by patients, and donations from donors.
Postcondition:
The record entered by the cashier is stored in the system and can be accessed by authorized personnel for future reference.
Business Rules:
 The system must comply with data protection laws and regulations.
The system must have a mechanism to authenticate the user before entering record data.
Implementation Constraints and Specifications:
The system must have a backup and recovery mechanism in case of data loss or system failure.
Assumptions:
The data entered by the cashier is accurate and complete.
The system is capable of storing and processing large amounts of data.
