Issue receipt

Description:
This use case involves generating receipts for a transaction paid in cash by a patient.
Precondition:
Patient has made a payment in cash.
Trigger:
Request to generate receipt for a cash transaction.
Typical Course of Events:
Actor
System response

 
 Step 1: The accountant logs onto the system to generate the receipt.
Step 3: The accountant enters the required details and confirms the information.
Step 5: The accountant prints the receipt and gives it to the patient.
 
 Step 2: The system prompts the accountant to enter the transaction details, including patient name, payment amount, and date of transaction.
Step 4: The system generates a receipt with the transaction details and a unique receipt number after validation of the entered information.
Step 6: The system updates the database with the receipt details.
 
Alternate Courses:
 Alternate to step 4: If the accountant enters incorrect information, the system prompts them to re-enter the correct details before generating the receipt.
Alternate to step 5: If there is a problem with the printer, the accountant can choose to save the receipt as a PDF and print it later.
Conclusion:
The system generates a receipt for a cash transaction made by a patient, which includes the transaction details and a unique receipt number
Postcondition:
The patient has a physical receipt for their cash transaction. The database is updated with the receipt details.
Business Rules:
Only authorized personnel can issue receipts and record transactions.
Implementation Constraints and Specifications:
The system must have a functional printer to generate the receipt.
Assumptions:
The patient is present at the time of the transaction and requests a receipt.
